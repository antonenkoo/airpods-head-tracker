04 — origin story

Born at 3 a.m. in Tokyo

Tokyo · winter · deadline

A rented desk in Nakameguro. Our developer was shipping a 3 a.m. build, folded over the laptop like a question mark, when a thought arrived through the AirPods: these things track my head for spatial audio — and nobody ever uses that data.

11 countries · 18 months

The first prototype was written before sunrise. Then it travelled: debugged on night trains to Kyoto, calibrated in Lisbon cafés, stress-tested on a ferry crossing the Bosphorus. One obsession the whole way: your posture deserves a guardian that lives in your ears.

Seoul · the sound & the color

In a Seoul PC-bang the posture guardian found its voice — a synth chirp borrowed from an arcade cabinet two seats away. And the green? That is the exact shade of a pharmacy cross reflected in the wet asphalt of Shibuya crossing at midnight. Some colors you don’t pick. They pick you.

Home · release day

Eighteen months later the app came home weighing 1.8 megabytes. No investors, no analytics, no launch party. Just one metric that mattered: the developer’s neck stopped hurting. Yours can too.

No servers. No subscriptions. Just physics, math and a small green cube.

05 — philosophy

Why it matters

You will spend roughly 80,000 hours of your life in front of a screen. The way you sit through them quietly becomes the way you feel — focus, energy, even mood follow the spine.

We can’t make you sit straight. But we can make your own ears watch over you — gently, offline, with a small green cube that never judges. It only reminds.

08 — faq

    
Questions, answered like a changelog

    

      

        
        
AirPods Pro (1st & 2nd gen), AirPods Max and AirPods 3+ — anything with Spatial Audio head tracking. Regular AirPods 2 don’t carry the motion sensor.

      

      

        
        
The motion sensors already run for Spatial Audio whenever your AirPods are on. Reading their stream adds nothing measurable — neither to the AirPods nor to your Mac.

      

      

        
        
Everything runs on localhost. No accounts, no analytics, no network calls. The only thing that ever leaves your Mac is sound from your speakers.

      

      

        
        
Because posture shouldn’t be a subscription. The code is open under MIT — read it, fork it, break it, fix it.

      

      

        
        
macOS 14 Sonoma or newer, Apple Silicon or Intel. AirPods must be connected as the audio output of the Mac.

      

    

  






  

    
09 — install

    
Running in a minute

    

      
01
Download the DMG and drag AirPodsTracker into Applications.

      
02
First launch: right-click → Open → Open. On macOS 15 also allow it in System Settings → Privacy & Security.

      
03
Allow Motion & Fitness access — that is the head-tracking sensor permission.

      
04
Put your AirPods on, look straight and press Calibrate. Done.

    

    

      Why the warning on first launch?
      The app is open source and signed without a paid Apple Developer ID, so Gatekeeper shows a one-time warning. If right-click → Open doesn’t help, run:
      
xattr -cr /Applications/AirPodsTracker.app
