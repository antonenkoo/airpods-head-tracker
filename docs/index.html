AirPods Head Tracker
for macOS

Real head tracking from your AirPods Pro / Max — right on your Mac. 3D visualization, posture monitoring and hands-free music control. No browser hacks: a native app reads the motion sensors directly.

⬇ Download for Mac (.dmg) macOS 14+ · Apple Silicon · 1.6 MB

Everything in one window

Put on your AirPods, launch the app — the cube mirrors your head in real time.

What it does

Your AirPods already have motion sensors for Spatial Audio. This app puts them to work.

🧊

Live 3D head tracking

Yaw, pitch and roll streamed from the headphones and rendered as a 3D cube with degree readouts.

🪑

Posture monitor

Slouching towards the screen? The app notices your head tilting down and gently reminds you with a sound.

🎵

Music by head gestures

Tilt your head to switch tracks in Spotify or Apple Music — hands stay on the keyboard.

🔊

Volume by head motion

Optional: nod or turn to nudge the system volume up and down. Fully configurable thresholds.

Install in a minute

Download the DMG and drag AirPodsTracker into Applications.

First launch: right-click the app → Open → Open. On macOS 15 also allow it in System Settings → Privacy & Security → “Open Anyway”.

Allow Motion & Fitness access when macOS asks — that’s the head-tracking sensor permission.

Put on your AirPods, look straight ahead and press “Calibrate”. Done.

Why the warning on first launch? The app is open source and signed without a paid Apple Developer ID, so Gatekeeper shows a one-time warning. If right-click → Open doesn’t help, run:
xattr -cr /Applications/AirPodsTracker.app

Requirements

macOS 14 Sonoma or newer AirPods Pro (1/2), AirPods Max, AirPods 3+ AirPods connected as audio output
